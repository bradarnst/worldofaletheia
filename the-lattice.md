## The Lattice

The **Lattice** is the underlying structure that holds reality together in Aletheia. It is not a god, not a mind, and not a hidden ruler of the world. Instead, it is the **framework** within which matter, life, magic, and even the gods themselves exist.

Everything is *in* the Lattice.\
Nothing exists *outside* it.

If reality were a tapestry, the Lattice would be the weave — not the picture, not the thread, but the thing that allows both to exist without falling apart.

***

### What the Lattice Is (and Is Not)

The Lattice is **structured but flexible**.

It is:

*   Stable enough that the world does not unravel
*   Flexible enough that the world can change

It is *not*:

*   Perfectly ordered
*   Perfectly chaotic
*   Frozen in time

A small amount of chaos is not a flaw in the Lattice — it is a **feature**. Without it, nothing new could ever happen. Without change, life would stagnate. With too much change, nothing would hold together long enough to matter.

Most scholars agree that the Lattice sits somewhere between these extremes, rather like a river: contained by its banks, but never still.

***

### The Lattice and Creation

According to nearly every surviving cosmology, the Lattice existed **before** anything else.

Before stone, before stars, before souls — there was structure. Not a plan, exactly, but **possibility arranged in a way that could persist**.

From this arrangement, the first stable patterns emerged. The most famous of these was **the Unicorn**.

The Unicorn is widely believed to have been the **first self-aware pattern** within the Lattice — not a creator, but the first being capable of recognising the harmony it existed within. From this moment, other stable patterns followed, eventually giving rise to the pantheon of gods.

In this understanding:

*   The gods did not create the Lattice
*   The Lattice did not obey the gods
*   Both arose together, in sequence, through emergence rather than command

Even most scholarly clerics now accept that the gods work *through* the Lattice, not above it — though they may phrase this more carefully when speaking to the faithful.

***

### Life, Death, and Return

Living beings are best understood as **temporary patterns** within the Lattice.

They hold themselves together for a time — through will, biology, and a bit of luck — and then, at death, they gradually come apart again.

This is not sudden.\
This is not violent.\
And it is not erasure.

Instead, the essence of a being loosens and **returns to the wider structure**, where it contributes to future change. Identity fades, but influence does not vanish. Death is not a wall; it is a **dissolving boundary**.

Or, as one dry-humoured philosopher put it:

“Nothing is lost. It’s just badly sorted.”

***

### The Lattice and Magic

All magic works by interacting with the Lattice.

Magic does not break reality, replace it, or cheat it. It works by:

*   Sensing existing flows
*   Nudging them
*   Redirecting them slightly

Spells are highly stable. A spell that works today will work the same way for generations. Grimoires do not quietly go out of date, and apprentices do not wake up to find yesterday’s magic “obsolete.”

What *does* change — slowly — is **lattice geography**.

Some places are better suited to certain kinds of magic than others, much like some soil grows wheat better than grapes. Over decades or centuries, these qualities can drift. A site may grow stronger, weaker, or differently “flavoured.”

This is usually subtle. Most people never notice it at all.

***

### Lattice Flows and the Pace of Change

Although the Lattice underlies everything, it is not uniform. In many places it can be sensed as **flow**: a tendency, pressure, or ease that favours certain kinds of change over others.

These flows are not currents in the usual sense. Nothing is rushing past, and nothing can be “drained.” A better comparison is to wind patterns or groundwater: invisible, persistent, and influential, but slow to change.

#### Local Flows

At any given location, the Lattice may exhibit:

*   Stronger or weaker overall flow
*   A tendency toward certain kinds of magic
*   Greater ease, efficiency, or stability for particular workings

Such characteristics are often subtle. A wizard may notice that spells feel “lighter” here, or that a certain kind of magic requires less effort than expected. A Resonant might describe the same place as “louder,” “warmer,” or “easier to listen to.”

Most people never notice anything at all.

#### How Flows Change

Under normal circumstances, changes in lattice flow are **slow and incremental**.

*   Minor shifts can occur over **years or decades**
*   More noticeable changes take **centuries**
*   Fundamental transformations occur only over **millennia**

A place that favours one kind of magic may, over many lifetimes, gradually favour another. Even then, the change is rarely dramatic. A marsh does not become a desert overnight; it dries, floods, shifts, and settles long before it becomes something else.

The important point is this:

**The Lattice changes by drift, not by lurch.**

#### Observing Change

Only those who:

*   Study the same location for many years
*   Keep careful records
*   Or possess very long lives

are likely to notice clear patterns of change.

Scholars compare this work to studying old maps of rivers or coastlines. One drawing looks much like another. Only when many are laid side by side does movement become obvious.

Most magical traditions accept that flows change, but few agree on *why*. Some blame long-term natural cycles, others the slow accumulation of magical activity, and still others the quiet influence of living beings themselves.

#### What Does Not Change

Despite shifting flows:

*   Spell structures remain stable
*   Spell effects remain consistent
*   Magical knowledge does not “expire”

A spell does not stop working because the Lattice has changed. At most, it may become slightly easier or harder to cast in a particular place, or lose the extra strength it once enjoyed there.

True changes to how magic works require **extraordinary events** and are so rare that most scholars treat them as theoretical.

#### The Cataclysm as an Outlier

The Cataclysm stands apart because it forced an enormous amount of change into a very short time.

Flows near the epicentre shifted abruptly, overlapping and interfering in unfamiliar ways. Many practitioners found that spells they had known for years suddenly felt wrong, unstable, or unreachable — not because the spells themselves had changed, but because the surrounding lattice context had.

This was shocking precisely because it **does not normally happen**.

In time, the flows settled again.

#### In Brief

*   Lattice flows exist and can be sensed
*   They change slowly and predictably
*   Short-term volatility is abnormal
*   Long-term drift is natural
*   Spellcraft remains stable throughout

Or, as one often-quoted field note puts it:

*“The ground may rise or sink, but the language of walking does not change.”*Chaos, Stability, and the Cataclysm

Chaos is always present in the Lattice, but it is usually **well behaved**.

The Cataclysm was exceptional because it forced far too much change into far too little time. Near its epicentre, lattice patterns twisted and overlapped in unfamiliar ways. Elsewhere, the effects were temporary and faded as stability returned.

The important point is this:

*   The Lattice was not broken
*   No permanent wounds remain
*   What persists is complexity, not damage

The world recovered — though it remembers.

### The Talent (Brief Note)

Some beings possess a heightened ability to sense and work with the Lattice. This capacity is commonly called **the Talent**.

The Talent:

*   Exists in degrees
*   Is never simply “on” or “off”
*   Is expressed differently by wizards, clerics, druids, and others

Because the Talent is so closely tied to lived experience, it is treated in a separate article.

### Ongoing Questions

Scholars still argue about:

*   Whether the Lattice should be described as mechanical or ecological
*   How much influence powerful beings can exert on lattice geography
*   Whether the Unicorn was unique or merely first

These debates are considered a sign that the subject is alive, rather than settled.

***

### In Summary

The Lattice is not something to defeat, command, or escape.
