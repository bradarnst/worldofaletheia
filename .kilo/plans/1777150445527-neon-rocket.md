# Open-Source-First Promotion and Monetization Exploration

## Goal

Explore how World of Aletheia can create direct or indirect value without compromising its identity as an open-source, open-access fantasy worldbuilding and campaign platform.

This plan is intentionally **not** an implementation plan for automatic website changes. Visible commercial, donation-oriented, service-oriented, sponsorship-oriented, or product-oriented changes require separate review and explicit approval before implementation. However, near-term website changes are acceptable if meaningful proof of interest is gathered first and the proposed change respects the open-source boundary.

## Core Constraint

World of Aletheia should remain open-source-first, with the Canon of the World of Aletheia kept open as the permanent core.

Near-term promotion should make the project easier to discover, understand, trust, and discuss. It should not make the site feel like a sales funnel, donation funnel, SaaS teaser, or portfolio landing page unless that direction is deliberately approved later or justified by evidence of commercial/community interest.

Practical implications:

- Do not add services pages, support pages, pricing pages, donation CTAs, campaign-platform waitlists, sponsorship placements, advertising placements, or commercial CTAs without explicit approval.
- Do not reposition the public site primarily around Brad's professional services without explicit approval.
- Do not introduce paid-product language until there is a clearer open-source plus value-add model or validated proof of interest.
- Keep monetization exploration mostly off-site, private, or low-visibility until the product and community direction is validated, unless a specific public test is approved.
- Treat campaign-platform interest as a research topic before treating it as a public product promise.
- Keep Canon open-source/open-access as the stable center. Campaign services, hosted/private campaign tooling, sponsorships, advertising, paid support, and add-on products may be treated more flexibly if they do not close the Canon.

## Strategic Frame

The strongest current strategy is not immediate monetization. It is audience, credibility, and option-building.

The site can support future revenue in several ways:

- As an open-source showcase of a serious Astro/Cloudflare publishing and campaign platform.
- As a public worldbuilding project that attracts readers, contributors, artists, and collaborators.
- As a reference implementation for future campaign-platform tooling.
- As indirect proof of expertise for consulting or architecture work, without turning the site itself into a services site.
- As a possible foundation for a future open-core or value-add product, if validated.

The near-term objective is therefore:

> Grow qualified attention and trust around the project while preserving open-source credibility and avoiding premature commercialization.

## Open-Core Boundary

The default commercial model should be open-core/open-source-friendly rather than closed-product-first.

Permanent open core:

- Canon world content and public lore reference surfaces.
- The identity of Aletheia as an open worldbuilding project.
- The public-facing value of the site as a readable campaign/world archive.
- Core code and documentation, subject to the final licensing decision.

Flexible commercial layers:

- Hosted/private campaign spaces.
- Managed campaign imports or bespoke campaign-site setup.
- Paid operational support, custom deployment, or consulting.
- Sponsorships or advertising, if appropriate and clearly separated from Canon integrity.
- Premium media/storage/collaboration features if a hosted campaign platform emerges.
- Packaged products such as PDFs, map packs, or curated releases, if compatible with the open Canon.

Guardrail:

- Commercial layers should add convenience, hosting, support, production value, or private/collaborative capability. They should not make the open Canon feel incomplete, bait-and-switched, or artificially restricted.

## Current Leverage Points

The site already has assets that can support promotion and future monetization without adding sales-oriented pages:

- Public lore, systems, campaigns, references, maps, calendar, and timeline surfaces.
- SEO infrastructure: canonical URLs, sitemap, robots controls, Open Graph metadata, production hostname logic, and `astro-seo`.
- Contact and contribute flows that already exist and can remain general-purpose.
- Public/protected campaign access, authentication, and campaign memberships, which are useful validation assets for future campaign tooling.
- Technical architecture story: Astro 6, Cloudflare, R2/S3-style object content, D1/auth, full-text search, Obsidian-first publishing, static/server hybrid deployment.
- GitHub visibility that can support open-source credibility and technical discovery.

## Recommended Positioning

The site should continue to be positioned primarily as a world and campaign archive, not a business.

Recommended public-facing position:

> World of Aletheia is an open fantasy worldbuilding and campaign archive, built as a modern Astro/Cloudflare publishing platform and evolving toward richer campaign-management tools.

This leaves room for several audiences without forcing monetization language:

- RPG/worldbuilding audience: readers, GMs, writers, artists, contributors, players.
- Open-source/technical audience: developers, architects, Astro users, Cloudflare users, Obsidian workflow users.
- Future product audience: GMs or worldbuilders who might someday want similar campaign tooling.
- Professional credibility audience: people who infer Brad's capabilities from the project without the project directly selling services.

## Revenue Paths to Keep Open

### Commercial Activation Triggers

Commercial or product-oriented website changes can move from deferred to near-term if one or more validation signals appear.

Strong signals:

- A serious inbound inquiry asks for help building, hosting, or adapting a similar site.
- A GM/group asks to use the campaign platform for their own campaign.
- Someone explicitly asks how to sponsor, donate, commission, advertise, or support the project.
- A community post produces repeated interest in the tooling rather than only the lore.
- Multiple people ask for self-hosting instructions, templates, or a managed version.
- A collaborator, artist, writer, or publisher proposes a concrete partnership.
- Analytics show sustained traffic to campaign/tooling/technical pages from qualified sources.

Moderate signals:

- GitHub stars/forks/issues/discussions indicate technical reuse interest.
- Repeated questions appear around Obsidian-to-Astro publishing, Cloudflare hosting, campaign permissions, or search.
- RPG/community feedback identifies a recurring pain point that Aletheia already solves.
- A small number of trusted GMs express willingness to pilot the platform.

Weak signals:

- Generic compliments.
- One-off curiosity without follow-up.
- Traffic spikes from non-qualified sources.
- Interest only in consuming the lore, with no platform/support/product signal.

Activation rule:

- Strong signals can justify immediate planning for site changes.
- Moderate signals justify further validation or a limited public test.
- Weak signals should not trigger monetization changes.

Possible near-term changes after activation:

- A campaign-platform interest page.
- A lightweight pilot inquiry path.
- A technical case study that explains the platform.
- A sponsorship/support route if people ask for one.
- A services or custom-build page if inbound commercial interest is real.
- Sponsored/advertising placements only if they are tasteful, relevant, and do not compromise Canon trust.

### Path 1: Indirect Professional Credibility

This remains the strongest near-term economic path, but it does **not** require adding services pages to World of Aletheia now.

How to pursue without changing the site into a sales funnel:

- Use the GitHub repo, LinkedIn posts, case-study posts elsewhere, conference-style writeups, or a separate personal portfolio if desired.
- Reference World of Aletheia as an example project when contextually appropriate.
- Keep the public site focused on the world and platform rather than explicit professional services.

Future optional website changes, requiring approval:

- A low-key technical architecture page.
- A link to an external personal/professional site.
- A case study that explains the build without selling services.
- A services page only if the site direction later supports that.

Priority: High as an indirect opportunity, low for immediate site changes.

### Path 2: Open-Source Donations or Sponsorship

Donations can fit an open-source project, but they can also change the perceived tone of the site. This should be deferred until there is a clearer support story or until people explicitly ask for a way to support the project.

Possible future forms:

- GitHub Sponsors if the technical project becomes useful to others.
- Ko-fi if community members ask for a simple support route.
- Open Collective only if governance/community contribution becomes more formal.

Guiding principle:

- Donations should support the open project, not create a feeling that readers are being monetized.

Future optional website changes, requiring approval and ideally a validation signal:

- A subtle GitHub Sponsors link from the repository rather than the lore site.
- A low-key support note on an About or GitHub page.
- A support page only if there is community demand or a clear open-source funding model.

Priority: Low to Medium, trigger-based.

### Path 3: Contributor, Writer, and Artist Network

This is more important than immediate money. Contributors can increase content quality, breadth, reach, and word of mouth.

Near-term approach:

- Keep contribution curated.
- Focus first on corrections, feedback, continuity issues, and informal collaboration.
- Do not make broad public calls for writers or artists until licensing, attribution, canon authority, and review process are clear.

Future optional website changes, requiring approval:

- Contributor guidelines.
- Licensing/attribution policy.
- Clear categories for corrections, writing proposals, art/map proposals, technical contributions, and campaign reports.
- Contributor credits once real contributors exist.

Important cautions:

- Clarify IP/licensing before accepting creative work.
- Keep canon authority centralized unless governance intentionally changes.
- Avoid unpaid-labor ambiguity.

Priority: Medium.

### Path 4: Campaign Platform Product

This is the most plausible direct product path, but it is not validated yet and should not be publicly promised prematurely.

Potential future product shape:

- Open-source campaign archive/publishing platform.
- Optional hosted/value-add paid version later.
- Obsidian/import-first campaign publishing.
- Public/private campaign pages.
- Session logs, timelines, NPCs, factions, places, maps, permissions, search, relationship navigation.
- Optional worldbuilding layers for homebrew settings.

Validation should happen before public productization:

1. Continue using Aletheia as the reference implementation.
2. Document recurring pain points from actual use.
3. Privately discuss the concept with trusted GMs/worldbuilders.
4. Offer a manually assisted pilot only if there is serious interest.
5. Define what remains open-source and what, if anything, could be paid value-add.

Possible open-source plus value-add split later:

- Open-source: codebase, self-hosting, public campaign archive features, basic import/export, documentation.
- Paid value-add: hosted infrastructure, private campaign hosting, managed imports, premium storage/media, support, collaboration features, backups, custom domain setup, bespoke configuration.

Future optional website changes, requiring approval:

- Campaign-platform interest form.
- Pilot-program page.
- Hosted-version routes or pricing pages only after validation and open-core boundaries are clear.

Priority: Medium to Long Term.

### Path 5: Publishing, PDFs, and Setting Products

Open content makes direct publishing less straightforward, but there may still be value in curated packages.

Potential future products:

- Pay-what-you-want PDFs.
- Print-on-demand setting guides.
- Map packs.
- Adventure starters or campaign arcs.
- System-neutral setting books.

Recommended approach:

- Do not lead with this.
- First learn which content readers actually care about.
- Only package content once the setting has clear demand and a coherent release candidate.

Priority: Low to Medium.

### Path 6: Ads and Affiliate Revenue

This is currently the weakest fit for Canon pages, but sponsorship or advertising could be viable around campaign/platform surfaces if it is relevant, transparent, and does not compromise trust.

Avoid for Canon and lore-first surfaces:

- Display ads.
- Intrusive affiliate links.
- Generic sponsorship language.

Possible later use after approval or sponsor interest:

- Highly relevant affiliate links or sponsorships only if they serve readers and do not undermine trust.
- Clearly labeled sponsorship for campaign tools, maps, hosting, art, or technical infrastructure.
- Advertising around campaign-platform or resource pages if the audience and placement make sense.

Priority: Low.

## Promotion Plan

Promotion can move forward before monetization, as long as it promotes the open project rather than a commercial funnel.

### SEO Foundation

These are mostly discovery and quality tasks, not monetization tasks:

- Verify Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools.
- Submit `https://worldofaletheia.com/sitemap.xml`.
- Confirm sitemap includes intended public pages and excludes protected/noindex content.
- Confirm no accidental `noindex` on public production content.
- Review Open Graph previews so shared links look credible.
- Improve page titles/descriptions where they undersell the content.
- Consider structured data only where it accurately describes existing content.

SEO content themes:

- Fantasy worldbuilding.
- Tabletop RPG campaign archive.
- GURPS campaign resources.
- Fantasy calendar/worldbuilding calendar.
- Obsidian publishing workflow.
- Astro/Cloudflare open-source content platform.
- Static/server hybrid sites with authenticated content.

### Content-Led Promotion

The safest promotion strategy is to publish useful, non-sales content.

Potential content formats:

- Worldbuilding notes: design choices, cultures, maps, history, ecology, cosmology.
- Campaign archive notes: how campaign logs are organized, what is public vs protected, lessons from play.
- Technical build notes: Astro, Cloudflare, R2, D1, auth, search, content sync, Obsidian workflow.
- Reference feature spotlights: calendar, timeline, maps, search, relationship navigation.
- Project updates: what changed and why, without turning updates into marketing copy.

Cadence suggestion:

- One substantial post/update per month if sustainable.
- Short social/forum updates only when there is something genuinely useful to share.

### Community and Directory Promotion

Promote in communities by contributing useful context, not by dropping links.

RPG/worldbuilding communities:

- Worldbuilding forums and subreddits where self-promotion is allowed.
- GURPS forums and communities when sharing relevant resources.
- RPGGeek, RPG.net, EN World, and similar forums when a post solves a real problem or shares useful material.
- Obsidian TTRPG communities for workflow discussions.

Technical communities:

- Astro Discord/showcase if the technical implementation is documented well.
- Cloudflare developer communities for platform architecture writeups.
- Dev.to, Hashnode, LinkedIn, or a separate technical blog for architecture notes.
- GitHub README/discussions if the open-source side becomes easier for others to use.

Directory/showcase opportunities:

- Astro showcase or examples.
- Cloudflare developer examples if a technical article is strong enough.
- Obsidian workflow showcases.
- RPG resource directories after public content is mature enough.

### Social Media Plan

Use social media to share the project, not to sell from the project.

Recommended channels:

- LinkedIn for technical architecture and open-source lessons.
- Mastodon/Bluesky for worldbuilding snippets, screenshots, maps, project updates.
- Reddit/forums for targeted, high-effort posts tailored to each community.
- YouTube or short video only if walkthroughs become worth the production effort.

Post types:

- Technical breakdowns of specific implementation choices.
- Lore snippets with links to relevant pages.
- Map/calendar/timeline feature spotlights.
- Campaign organization lessons.
- Open-source progress notes.

### Paid Promotion

Do not use paid promotion now.

Paid ads should only be reconsidered if there is a clear objective and a non-intrusive destination:

- Grow readership for a major public release.
- Test interest in campaign-platform tooling.
- Promote an open-source launch.
- Drive traffic to an external professional site, if one exists.

Prerequisites before any paid promotion:

- Clear hypothesis.
- Analytics.
- Appropriate landing destination.
- Explicit approval.

## Website Change Policy

No website changes should be treated as automatic outcomes of this plan. Commercial changes are allowed near-term only when they pass both gates: a validation signal exists and the specific change is reviewed/approved.

Allowed without additional monetization approval:

- General SEO quality improvements that do not alter positioning toward monetization.
- Fixing incorrect metadata, broken social previews, sitemap issues, or noindex mistakes.
- Improving existing content clarity without adding commercial CTAs.
- Improving GitHub/open-source documentation if separately approved as documentation work.

Requires explicit review and approval:

- Services or `Work With Me` pages.
- Support, donation, sponsor, or patron pages.
- Pricing or paid-version pages.
- Campaign-platform interest/waitlist forms.
- Paid pilot or hosted offering pages.
- Footer/header commercial CTAs.
- Professional repositioning of About or Contact.
- Contributor guidelines that define licensing or contribution rights.

Can move quickly after validation:

- Add a campaign-platform interest page after serious GM/group interest or repeated platform questions.
- Add a sponsor/support route after explicit support, donation, sponsorship, or commission inquiries.
- Add a services/custom-build route after a serious inbound commercial inquiry.
- Add advertising/sponsorship placements after a relevant sponsor opportunity, with clear separation from Canon content.
- Add hosted/private campaign offering pages after pilot interest and open-core boundaries are documented.

Non-negotiable boundary:

- Canon content remains open-source/open-access. Any paid or sponsored layer must be additive rather than restrictive.

## Research and Validation Plan

### Phase 1: Baseline Discovery

Purpose: understand whether people are finding and using the site.

Actions:

- Review Search Console/Bing data once available.
- Identify top landing pages and queries.
- Review GitHub traffic/stars/forks if available.
- Track referrals from communities where the project is shared.
- Note which pages earn organic interest without promotion.

Default outcome: no public site monetization changes in this phase. Exception: if a strong commercial/support/platform signal appears, draft a targeted website-change proposal for approval.

### Phase 2: Promotion Experiments

Purpose: learn which audiences respond.

Actions:

- Share one technical architecture writeup off-site or in GitHub documentation.
- Share one worldbuilding/campaign post in a relevant RPG/worldbuilding community.
- Share one Obsidian/Astro/Cloudflare workflow post in a technical community.
- Track which topic produces the best engagement and quality feedback.

No commercial CTAs required. If promotion produces strong validation, prepare a narrowly scoped follow-up change such as an interest page, pilot inquiry path, or support/sponsor route.

### Phase 3: Campaign Platform Validation

Purpose: learn whether the campaign platform idea has real demand.

Actions:

- Privately interview or message a few trusted GMs/worldbuilders.
- Ask what tools they use now, what hurts, what they would self-host, and what they would pay for.
- Compare demand for open-source self-hosting vs managed hosting vs bespoke setup.
- Identify whether the hard problem is content import, permissions, search, maps, session logs, relationship navigation, or collaboration.

No public waitlist unless approved later. If conversations reveal serious demand, the next step can be an approved lightweight interest page rather than waiting for a complete SaaS plan.

### Phase 4: Open-Core/Product Boundary Definition

Purpose: define the viable paid layer without undermining the open-source project.

Questions to answer:

- What is always open-source?
- What is free hosted, if anything?
- What value-add could reasonably be paid?
- What support burden would paid users create?
- Is this a product, consulting offer, managed hosting offer, or simply an open-source project?

Possible outcomes:

- Continue as open-source only.
- Add donations/sponsorship.
- Offer bespoke campaign sites manually.
- Build a hosted version.
- Keep Aletheia as portfolio/proof-of-work only.
- Add commercial surfaces to the site once the open-core boundary and trigger evidence are documented.

## Metrics

Promotion metrics:

- Organic search impressions and clicks.
- Top landing pages.
- Backlinks/referrals.
- Social/forum referral traffic.
- GitHub stars, forks, issues, or discussions.
- Quality of inbound comments or contribution inquiries.

Product validation metrics:

- Number of serious campaign-platform conversations.
- Repeated pain points across different GMs/groups.
- Willingness to self-host.
- Willingness to pay for hosting or setup.
- Requests for private campaign access, search, import/export, maps, relationship views, or collaboration.

Economic signal metrics:

- Direct inquiries that mention the site as proof of capability.
- Requests to reuse or adapt the platform.
- Requests for help building similar sites.
- Voluntary offers to donate, contribute, sponsor, or collaborate.

## Revised 90-Day Roadmap

### Days 1-15: Clarify Direction and Activation Criteria

- Confirm open-source-first positioning.
- Decide which parts of the repo/content are open-source and under what license.
- Identify whether technical promotion should happen on GitHub, LinkedIn, a separate blog, or the main site later.
- Review existing SEO/search visibility baseline.
- Define the evidence threshold for adding campaign-platform, sponsorship, support, advertising, or services surfaces.

### Days 16-30: SEO and Shareability Review

- Verify webmaster tools and sitemap submission.
- Review metadata/social previews.
- Identify low-risk SEO improvements that do not add commercial positioning.
- Draft candidate structured-data improvements for review before implementation.

### Days 31-60: Non-Commercial Promotion Experiments

- Share one useful technical writeup externally or in the repository.
- Share one worldbuilding/campaign feature in an appropriate RPG/worldbuilding space.
- Track engagement and feedback quality.
- Avoid service/support/product CTAs unless an approved validation-trigger test is being run.

### Days 61-90: Campaign Platform Discovery

- Conduct private validation conversations with trusted GMs/worldbuilders.
- Document desired features and pain points.
- Draft an open-source vs value-add boundary proposal.
- Decide whether a public campaign-platform interest surface is warranted.
- If proof of commercial interest exists, draft the first approved commercial website change.

## Decisions Needed Before Any Implementation

- What license applies to code, content, maps, art, and contributed material?
- Should technical writeups live on World of Aletheia, GitHub, LinkedIn, or a separate personal site?
- Should World of Aletheia ever include donation/support links, or should support remain GitHub-only if used?
- Should professional services be separated onto a different domain/site if pursued?
- What would count as enough validation to create a campaign-platform interest page?
- What value-add features could be paid without weakening the open-source promise?
- What kinds of sponsorship or advertising would be acceptable, and where would they be prohibited?
- Which campaign-related features are part of the open core versus a hosted/value-add layer?
- What exact proof of commercial interest should trigger immediate website changes?

## Recommended Default Direction

The recommended default is conditional rather than purely conservative:

- Keep World of Aletheia open-source-first, with Canon permanently open.
- Promote the project through useful content, community participation, and technical credibility.
- Treat monetization as option-building until validation appears.
- Move quickly on website changes if strong proof of commercial, sponsorship, support, or campaign-platform interest appears.
- Require explicit review and approval before any commercial, donation, service, sponsorship, advertising, or waitlist feature appears on the site.
- Keep paid/value-add layers additive and outside the open Canon.

This preserves trust while keeping future paths open: open-source community, contributor growth, indirect professional credibility, donations, sponsorships, advertising, bespoke consulting, and a potential open-core campaign platform.
