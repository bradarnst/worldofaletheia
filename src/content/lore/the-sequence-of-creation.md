## The Lattice
The Lattice has always existed. It is the underlying condition of reality that allows structure, stability, limitation, and resonance. It does not act, intend, communicate, or judge. All forces, including divine power, operate within its constraints. The Lattice can be strained, damaged, or stabilized, but it cannot be persuaded or directed.

## Matter and the Early World
Through the Lattice, reality stabilized into enduring forms. Inorganic matter existed long before life. Forces, patterns, and cycles were present without intention or meaning. The world existed, but it did not yet renew itself in a sustained way.

## The Unicorn
The Unicorn emerged once material conditions and the Lattice allowed for persistent, self-renewing processes. She was sentient from the beginning, but not directive or hierarchical. She does not rule, command, or design. She embodies continuation, balance, and persistence without authority.

## Life
The potential for life existed within matter structured by the Lattice, but life was not inevitable. Certain flows and patterns could have produced life eventually, or not at all. The Unicorn intervened to ensure that life emerged and did so early. She did not create life from nothing but activated and stabilized the first self-sustaining cycles. Once life persisted on its own, she withdrew direct influence. Life diversified, adapted, and changed without further intervention.

## The Gods
As life spread and complexity increased, forces arose that could have crystallized into god-like entities over time, unpredictably and without limit. The Unicorn intervened to ensure that these forces emerged in stable, bounded forms. With the Lattice providing structure and constraint, the gods came into being as sentient, role-defined mediators rather than absolute rulers.
 
They were shaped from existing forces and stabilized so that complexity could be managed rather than be allowed to fracture the world. Once this stabilization was achieved, the Unicorn again withdrew direct influence.

## Mortals
With life established and the gods in place, the conditions for sentient, reflective mortality were present but not inevitable. The Unicorn intervened for a final time to ensure that such beings emerged and endured. She did not shape specific peoples, forms, or cultures. She ensured the conditions under which self-aware mortal life could persist, then stepped back.
 
Elves emerged first, followed later by dwarves, humans, hobbits, and other races.
