**Deirdre: The Binding Heart**  
Often called _Lady of Love_ or _She Who Makes Justice Worth Bearing_

**Domains and Aspects**

Love, devotion, loyalty, emotional bonds, compassion, chosen attachment

Common aspects include:

·       Love as commitment rather than indulgence

·       Devotion as a moral force

·       Loyalty that sustains justice

·       Affection that motivates sacrifice

Deirdre is invoked where bonds between people matter more than rules alone.

**Core Associations**

Romantic love, partnership, loyalty, forgiveness, beauty, emotional truth, reconciliation

Deirdre is associated with love that endures strain and survives conflict, not with fleeting desire.

**Worship and Devotion**

Deirdre is honoured by:

·       Lovers and partners

·       Spouses and families

·       Those who swear vows of loyalty

·       Those seeking reconciliation

She is commonly prayed to:

·       Before marriage or long-term commitment

·       When loyalty is tested

·       During separation or loss

·       When forgiveness is sought without denying harm

Offerings often take the form of vows, shared acts, or personal sacrifices made freely.

**Clergy and Champions**

Deirdre’s clergy serve as counsellors, witnesses to vows, and protectors of bonds.

Those devoted to her are expected to:

·       Respect consent and mutual devotion

·       Protect relationships from coercion or abuse

·       Support reconciliation without excusing wrongdoing

·       Uphold loyalty alongside honesty

Champions of Deirdre often work alongside those devoted to Corwin, tempering justice with compassion.

**Symbols and Iconography**

·       Interwoven cords

·       A knot that cannot be pulled apart without breaking

·       A pair of joined hands

·       Deep red and warm gold as common colours

Imagery emphasizes connection, tension, and strength through union.

**Shrines and Sacred Spaces**

Deirdre’s shrines are found:

·       In homes and shared spaces

·       At marriage sites

·       Near courts or temples of Corwin

They are intimate rather than grand, often shared rather than owned.

**Taboos and Prohibitions**

Followers of Deirdre avoid:

·       Forcing affection or devotion

·       Using love to excuse injustice

·       Breaking sworn bonds lightly

·       Confusing desire with commitment

Betrayal of trust is considered a serious moral failure.

**Status and Visibility**

Deirdre is widely honoured and deeply respected.

Her worship is common across all social classes and cultures. She is especially prominent wherever Corwin’s influence is strong.
