## Vialle: The Patient Hand

Often called _Lady of the Steady Craft_ or _Keeper of Enduring Works_


![Vialle potter](<../../assets/images/Vialle potter.png>)
**Domains and Aspects**

Wisdom, craftsmanship, patient creation, restoration, continuity
Common aspects include:

·       Wisdom as experience tested over time

·       Craftsmanship as care in execution

·       Patience as strength rather than delay

·       Restoration as sacred responsibility

Vialle is invoked where something must be built to last, repaired properly, or understood through long effort rather than sudden insight.

**Core Associations**

Skilled labour, repair, preservation, mentorship, tradition, generational knowledge

Vialle is associated with steady work and thoughtful design rather than brilliance or speed. She represents what endures beyond fashion or excitement.

**Worship and Devotion**

Vialle is honoured by:

·       Artisans and builders

·       Healers and caretakers

·       Scribes and preservers of knowledge

·       Those who restore what has been damaged

She is commonly prayed to:

·       Before beginning long work

·       During repair or reconstruction

·       When teaching a craft

·       When preserving family or cultural traditions

Offerings often take the form of completed work, careful restoration, or time devoted to teaching.

**Clergy and Champions**

Vialle’s clergy are teachers, restorers, and guardians of skilled practice.

Those devoted to her are expected to:

·       Work carefully rather than quickly

·       Preserve what is worth keeping

·       Teach without arrogance

·       Value durability over display

Champions of Vialle defend what communities have built and protect traditions that give stability.

**Symbols and Iconography**

A spindle or loom  
A chisel and stone  
Interlocking joints  
Earth tones, muted gold, and stone grey

Imagery emphasizes hands at work, unfinished pieces being shaped, and layered patterns built over time.

**Shrines and Sacred Spaces**

Vialle’s shrines are found:

·       In workshops and guild halls

·       In restoration houses

·       In schools of craft

·       In simple spaces devoted to careful labour

They are quiet, orderly places focused on practice rather than display.

**Taboos and Prohibitions**

Followers of Vialle avoid:

·       Careless or rushed work

·       Waste of materials

·       Destroying what can be repaired

·       Disregarding accumulated knowledge

Neglect is considered a moral failure.

**Status and Visibility**

Vialle is widely respected, though not always dramatically worshipped. Her presence is strongest in communities that value continuity and skilled labour.![Vialle potter](<../../assets/images/Vialle potter.png>)
